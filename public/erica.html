Dietitian learner Q&A
In the reintroduction phase, should we test one FODMAP subgroup at a time even if the patient suspects multiple triggers?
EatFit Training AIYes — this is one of the most important protocol points. You test one FODMAP subgroup at a time, even when multiple are suspected. Here's why:

• If you introduce fructans and lactose simultaneously and symptoms occur, you can't determine which triggered the response
• The reintroduction protocol is specifically designed to isolate variables
• Kings College London protocol (Monash-aligned) confirms: 3-day test per subgroup, 3-day wash between

Erica covers this in detail in Module 4 — particularly the patient communication aspect. Would you like me to pull up that section?
Yes please, and what about polyols — when do we test those?
EatFit Training AIPolyols (sorbitol and mannitol) are tested last in the standard protocol — they tend to cause osmotic diarrhea even at lower thresholds, so there's clinical value in establishing other subgroup responses first.

Module 4, Section 3 covers the polyol testing sequence. I've queued it for your next session. Your progress: Module 3 complete ✓ — 6 CPD points logged for ADSA.

Great question — this comes up a lot in practice. Erica will cover a case study in Module 5 where a patient had both sorbitol AND fructan sensitivity. Worth watching. 🎓
